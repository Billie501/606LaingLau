Billie-Jean Laing Personal Reflection and Recommendation related to Virtual Reality for use of Brain Damage Rehabilitation


Reflections

Ethical issues relating to the technology, your personal reflections and recommendations [Individual].

After researching Virtual Reality as a rehabilitation method for patients with traumatic brain injury, I believe a lot more research, studies and scholarly literature need to be conducted and written to give a clearer picture of its effectiveness. From an ethical viewpoint, there needs to be a better balance of sharing information for research purposes whilst keeping the integrity of a patient’s information private and confidential. It is not satisfactory for sensitive information to be leaked even if done so unintentionally. The leaking of private information could have a detrimental impact on the patient’s life, well-being and mental/emotional capacity and the life, well-being and mental/emotional capacity of those around the patient. For example, once the patient is rehabilitated and if they decide to contribute to society through employment, sports, or any other activity, there could be a bias around how their work is received in the community. This bias could either work in their favour or against their favour. Any situation where there is a bias provides an unfair and unfavourable situation for any parties involved (including colleagues and teammates.
